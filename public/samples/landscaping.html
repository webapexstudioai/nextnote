How we work

Slow design.
Built for decades, not seasons.

Our process is unhurried by design. We study your site through at least one full season before the first stone is set. The garden you get back will outlive the contract by forty years.

i.

Site Study

We walk the site through a full seasonal cycle — sun arc, drainage patterns, microclimates, soil pH at six points. A 32-page existing-conditions report.

ii.

Concept & Plan

Hand-drawn schematics, native-forward plant palette, materials sourced within 100 miles. Three concept iterations before construction begins.

iii.

Build & Plant

Our own crew — no subcontractors. Dry-stack stone, hand-mortared paths, plants installed with bone meal and compost from our own piles.

iv.

Stewardship

Two-year establishment program included with every install. Weekly visits, seasonal pruning, soil testing. We watch it grow with you.

Year-round care

A garden has four seasons.
So do we.

Existing-garden clients can subscribe to our seasonal stewardship program. Same crew, same hands, every visit — we know your garden by name.

Season I
March — May

Spring Awakening

  • Soil amendment & compost top-dressing
  • Spring perennial division & replanting
  • Hand-pruning shrubs & ornamental trees
  • Native seed broadcast in meadow areas
  • Drip irrigation startup & system audit
From $640 · per visit
Season II
June — August

Summer Tending

  • Weekly hand-weeding & deadheading
  • Edge re-cutting & mulch refresh
  • Drought-stress monitoring & deep watering
  • Pollinator habitat support
  • Mid-summer minor pruning
From $420 · per visit
Season III
September — November

Fall Layering

  • Bulb planting (spring color planning)
  • Selective leaf management (not full clearing)
  • Compost application & soil testing
  • Fall perennial cutbacks (selective)
  • Native seed collection & storage
From $580 · per visit
Season IV
December — February

Winter Architecture

  • Structural pruning of trees & shrubs
  • Garden plan revisions for next year
  • Storm damage cleanup & assessment
  • Stone & hardscape maintenance
  • Tool sharpening & off-season planning
From $380 · per visit
Studio philosophy

"We don't landscape. We work with sites long enough to learn what they want to become — and then we get out of the way and let them."

June Castellanos

Founder & Lead Designer · APLD

After fifteen years at Olin Studio in Philadelphia and another four restoring a five-acre property in the Willamette Valley, June founded Fernhouse with a single rule: every garden must be designed for the climate, soil, and stewardship realities of its specific square footage. No two projects look the same — and that's the point.

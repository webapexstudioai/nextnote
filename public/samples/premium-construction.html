The way we build

A two-year conversation between owner, architect, and the people who hold the chisel.

No general contractors. No managed subs. Every cabinet, every stone wall, every roof beam set by people who answer to us — and have for twenty years.

i.

Survey & Site Study

Six to twelve months observing the land before the first sketch. Solar arc, prevailing winds, watershed patterns, soil cores at every footing location. The house is found in the site, not imposed on it.

ii.

Architecture Collaboration

We work with a small roster of architects we've built alongside for twenty years — Studio B, Olson Kundig, CCY, Bohlin Cywinski Jackson — or whoever you choose. We don't design. We honor the design.

iii.

In-house Millwork & Stone

Our 14,000 sq ft Carbondale shop mills timber, fabricates cabinetry, and shapes hand-quarried stone. Same craftsmen on every project. Same standards on every joint.

iv.

Forty-year Stewardship

Every home we build comes with a written forty-year structural warranty and a lifetime promise to return for any callback, however small. We've serviced houses we built in 2008.

Søren learned timber framing from his grandfather in Telemark and apprenticed under master masons in Northern Italy before founding Halverson Build Co. with his brother Peter in Carbondale in 2003. The studio has stayed deliberately small — six homes a year — because the alternative would mean handing the work to people they don't know.

Materials & sourcing

a provenance.

We don't list "premium materials" in our quotes — we tell you the name of the mill, the quarry, the forge. Then we visit it together if you want to.

Reclaimed Timber

Douglas fir from 1903 Telluride mill, oak from Pennsylvania tobacco barns, salvaged longleaf heart pine.

M / 02

Yule Marble

Hand-quarried at 9,300 ft in Marble, CO — the same vein as the Lincoln Memorial.

Hand-Forged Hardware

Hinges, latches, and pulls forged at our partner blacksmith in Bozeman. Each piece signed.

Standing Seam Copper

20-oz copper from Revere Copper Products. Hand-soldered seams that will outlast the framing.

M / 05

Venetian Plaster

Hand-pulled lime plaster in the Marmorino tradition. Walls that breathe and warm with age.

M / 06

Drystack Stonework

Local Aspen sandstone & granite, set without mortar by a third-generation Welsh stonemason.
